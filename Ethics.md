**Mitigation for A10 (offensive content exposure):**
The sticker gallery in the app displays collected stickers as thumbnails. The researcher and any reviewers will limit time spent reviewing offensive content. If any individual feels distressed, they are encouraged to step away and seek support from a trusted adult or school counsellor. Flagged content will be handled promptly and removed from view after flagging.

**Mitigation for A11 (sensitive topic):**
Participants who have experienced cyberbullying may be emotionally affected by the subject matter. A brief information note will be shared with all participants before the study begins, including the contact details of a school counsellor or ChildLine South Africa (0800 055 555).

## Section B: Privacy and Data

| # | Question | Yes | No | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| B1 | Will any personal data be collected (names, phone numbers, location, images of identifiable people)? | | ✅ | Only sticker image hashes (SHA-256) and group names are stored — no personal identifiers |
| B2 | Will data be stored securely? | ✅ | | Data stored in Replit cloud environment with HTTPS encryption |
| B3 | Will data be shared with any third parties? | | ✅ | Data will not be shared outside the research project |
| B4 | Will data be deleted after the research is complete? | ✅ | | All collected sticker data and group records will be deleted at the end of the study period |
| B5 | Does the research involve accessing private messages, personal accounts, or private conversations without consent? | | ✅ | The bot only processes sticker media events in groups where it is a member; it does not read text messages or access private chats |

**Note on WhatsApp data:** The Anti-WA bot operates only in group chats where it has been added as a member by the group administrator. It does not access private chats, it does not read or store the text content of messages, and it only processes sticker file attachments. The bot's presence in each group is transparent to all members.
